We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled “LLM-Assisted ESG Disclosure Assessment for Taiwan Listed Companies” for consideration in *Business Strategy and the Environment*. This study explores how large language models (LLMs) can significantly improve the accuracy, consistency, and efficiency of ESG disclosure assessments within the bilingual and unique regulatory context of Taiwan-listed firms. We present a novel LLM-based framework, fine-tuned on Taiwan-specific ESG disclosures in both Mandarin and English, and provide empirical comparison against expert human evaluations demonstrating meaningful gains in assessment quality and speed.

The key contributions of our work are threefold: (1) a technical advancement introducing a tailored LLM framework that incorporates Taiwan’s regulatory and linguistic idiosyncrasies for ESG analysis; (2) an empirical validation showing that LLM-assisted assessment outperforms manual approaches in accuracy (7 percentage points improvement) and inter-rater reliability (Fleiss’ Kappa increase from 0.60 to 0.74), while cutting processing time by more than half; and (3) offering practical value by delivering a scalable, replicable tool to regulators, investors, and rating agencies that enhances ESG transparency and accountability in an important Asian market.

We believe *Business Strategy and the Environment* is the most appropriate venue for this research given its focus on innovative strategies, technologies, and business-environment interactions that advance sustainability and corporate responsibility. Our interdisciplinary approach—bridging AI technology with ESG disclosure practices—aligns with the journal’s audience of researchers and practitioners interested in improving environmental and social governance through novel, actionable solutions.

The data supporting our findings, including curated ESG reports and model code, are available upon reasonable request to promote reproducibility.
