# Research Concept — LLM-Assisted ESG Disclosure Assessment for Taiwan Listed Companies

# Research Concept Document

## 1. Research Question
How can large language models (LLMs) improve the accuracy, consistency, and timeliness of ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) disclosure assessments for Taiwan-listed companies?

## 2. Testable Hypothesis
Utilizing LLM-assisted ESG disclosure assessment will result in significantly higher accuracy and consistency compared to traditional manual evaluation methods for Taiwan-listed companies while also reducing the processing time required for such assessments.

## 3. Contributions

### Technical Contribution
- Development and validation of an LLM-based framework tailored to analyze ESG disclosures written in both Mandarin and English, incorporating Taiwan-specific regulatory and cultural contexts.

### Empirical Contribution
- Comparative analysis of ESG assessment accuracy and consistency between LLM-assisted and human expert evaluations for a sample of Taiwan-listed companies.
- Quantitative evidence on efficiency gains in ESG disclosure assessment timelines using LLM assistance.

### Practical Contribution
- Providing Taiwan regulators, investors, and rating agencies with a replicable, scalable tool to enhance ESG disclosure assessment reliability.
- Supporting improved ESG transparency and accountability among Taiwan-listed firms, aligning with global sustainability standards.

## 4. Literature Gaps / Limitations

1. **Limited focus on regional contexts:** Existing research predominantly targets ESG assessment in Western or global contexts, with insufficient examination of Taiwan's unique regulatory environment and language use.
2. **Manual assessment bottlenecks:** Most ESG disclosure evaluations rely heavily on manual review, leading to inconsistencies, bias, and delayed reporting, but few studies explore LLM-enabled automation to address these challenges.
3. **Lack of empirical validation:** There is a scarcity of empirical studies comparing AI/LLM-assisted ESG assessments with expert human judgments, particularly in markets with multilingual disclosures such as Taiwan.

## 5. Suggested Target Journal and Rationale

**Journal:** Business Strategy and the Environment

**Rationale:** This journal focuses on sustainability, corporate responsibility, and business-environment interactions, emphasizing innovative strategies and technologies to improve environmental and social governance. The proposed study aligns well by applying cutting-edge AI technology (LLMs) to advance ESG disclosure assessment practices within an important Asian market. The journal’s interdisciplinary audience of academics and practitioners will value both the technical innovation and practical implications of the research.
