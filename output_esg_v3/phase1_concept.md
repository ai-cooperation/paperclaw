# Research Concept — LLM-Assisted ESG Disclosure Assessment for Taiwan Listed Companies

# Research Concept Document

## Research Question
How can large language models (LLMs) enhance the accuracy and consistency of ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) disclosure assessment for Taiwan-listed companies?

## Hypothesis
The integration of LLM-based natural language processing techniques into ESG disclosure assessment improves the reliability, granularity, and timeliness of ESG scoring compared to traditional manual and rule-based approaches.

## Contributions

### Technical Contribution
- Development of an LLM-assisted ESG disclosure assessment framework tailored to the linguistic and regulatory context of Taiwan-listed companies, incorporating domain-specific fine-tuning and interpretability enhancements.

### Empirical Contribution
- Comprehensive evaluation of the LLM-assisted ESG assessment method using a dataset of ESG disclosures from Taiwan stock exchange-listed firms, demonstrating improvements in assessment accuracy and reduced inter-rater variability compared to existing scoring methods.

### Practical Contribution
- Provision of actionable insights and a scalable assessment tool for investors, regulators, and companies in Taiwan to better understand and communicate ESG performance, promoting transparency and facilitating sustainable investment decisions.

## Limitations in Existing Literature (Research Gaps)
1. **Limited Application of LLMs in ESG Assessment:** Existing ESG scoring methods for Taiwanese companies rely heavily on manual coding or shallow text analytics, with minimal use of advanced LLM techniques capable of nuanced context comprehension.
2. **Context-Specific ESG Disclosure Analysis:** Most ESG assessment frameworks are developed for Western markets, lacking validation or adaptation to Taiwan’s unique regulatory environment, language, and disclosure standards.
3. **Assessment Consistency and Transparency Challenges:** There is a scarcity of research addressing the reproducibility and interpretability of automated ESG disclosure assessments, limiting stakeholder trust and adoption.

## Suggested Target Journal
**Business Strategy and the Environment**

**Rationale:** This journal specializes in interdisciplinary research at the intersection of business strategy and environmental sustainability, with a strong focus on empirical and practical research related to ESG issues. It welcomes innovative methodological approaches—such as AI and LLM-based analyses—to advance understanding of corporate sustainability disclosures and reporting practices in diverse geographic contexts. The journal’s readership includes both academics and practitioners interested in sustainable business practices, aligning well with the proposed study’s contribution to ESG assessment in Taiwan.
