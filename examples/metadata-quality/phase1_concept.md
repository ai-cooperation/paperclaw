# Research Concept Document: Metadata Quality Across Academic Databases

## 1. Research Question
How does the quality of metadata vary across major academic databases, and what are the implications of these variations for research discoverability and bibliometric analyses?

## 2. Hypothesis
There are significant differences in metadata quality—measured by completeness, accuracy, consistency, and timeliness—across major academic databases, which affect the reliability of research discovery and the validity of bibliometric indicators derived from these sources.

## 3. Contributions

### Technical Contribution
- Development of a standardized framework and set of quantitative metrics for assessing metadata quality dimensions (completeness, accuracy, consistency, timeliness) across multiple academic databases.

### Empirical Contribution
- Comprehensive comparative analysis of metadata quality in leading academic databases (e.g., Web of Science, Scopus, PubMed, Google Scholar), providing detailed metrics and identifying patterns of discrepancies.

### Practical Contribution
- Recommendations for researchers, librarians, and database providers on best practices to enhance metadata quality and improve research visibility and evaluation accuracy.

## 4. Limitations in Existing Literature (Research Gaps)

1. **Lack of Standardized Metrics:** Existing studies often use inconsistent or non-comparable metrics to assess metadata quality, limiting cross-database comparison.

2. **Limited Scope:** Most prior research focuses on a single database or a small subset, insufficient for understanding broader systemic issues.

3. **Insufficient Focus on Impact:** Few studies explicitly link metadata quality variations to downstream effects on research discoverability and bibliometric analysis reliability.

## 5. Suggested Target Journal
**Scientometrics**

### Rationale
Scientometrics specializes in the quantitative study of science, technology, and innovation, including bibliometrics and research evaluation. It has a strong readership interested in metadata standards, data quality issues, and their impact on scholarly communication, making it an ideal venue for disseminating research on metadata quality across academic databases.
