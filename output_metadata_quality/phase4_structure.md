## 4. Research Contract

| Aspect              | Details                                                                                                                                                           |
|---------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Research Questions** | 1. How does metadata quality vary across major academic databases?<br>2. What are the measurable differences in completeness, accuracy, consistency, and timeliness?<br>3. How do these variations affect research discoverability and the validity of bibliometric indicators? |
| **Hypotheses**        | There exist statistically significant differences in metadata quality dimensions across databases, which lead to measurable impacts on the reliability of research discovery and bibliometric analyses. |
| **Methods**           | - Development of a standardized quantitative framework with explicit metrics<br>- Empirical cross-database data collection and assessment from Web of Science, Scopus, PubMed, and Google Scholar<br>- Statistical comparison using significance testing and correlation analyses<br>- Qualitative interpretation for downstream impacts |
| **Expected Outcomes** | - Quantitative evidence of metadata quality discrepancies<br>- Demonstration of correlation between metadata quality and research discoverability indicators<br>- Clear, standardized metrics and framework useful for future studies<br>- Practical recommendations for improving metadata quality |
| **Limitations**       | - Sampling constraints or access limitations given proprietary databases<br>- Potential variability in database update schedules affecting timeliness metric<br>- Challenges in verifying absolute accuracy due to lack of ground truth<br>- Focus on major databases may not generalize to niche or regional indexes |
