# Research Concept — LLM-Assisted ESG Disclosure Assessment for Taiwan Listed Companies

# Research Concept Document

## Topic
**LLM-Assisted ESG Disclosure Assessment for Taiwan Listed Companies**

## 1) Research Question
**How accurately and consistently can large language models (LLMs) assess the quality and completeness of ESG disclosures in annual and sustainability reports of Taiwan-listed companies compared with traditional disclosure scoring methods?**

A more specific version could be:

**To what extent do LLM-assisted evaluation methods improve the identification, classification, and scoring of ESG disclosure quality among Taiwan-listed firms, and what firm-level factors explain variation in disclosure quality?**

---

## 2) Testable Hypothesis
**H1:**  
*LLM-assisted ESG disclosure assessment produces disclosure quality scores that are significantly more aligned with expert human evaluations than conventional keyword-based or rule-based disclosure scoring methods.*

Optional additional hypotheses, if needed:

**H2:**  
*Taiwan-listed firms in environmentally sensitive industries exhibit higher ESG disclosure quality than firms in less environmentally sensitive industries.*

**H3:**  
*Firms with greater corporate governance strength and foreign investor ownership disclose ESG information more comprehensively and with higher specificity.*

---

## 3) Three Contributions

### A. Technical Contribution
Develops a **LLM-assisted framework for ESG disclosure assessment** that goes beyond keyword counting by evaluating semantic completeness, specificity, consistency, and relevance across ESG narratives in Chinese- and/or bilingual-language reports.

### B. Empirical Contribution
Provides **systematic evidence on ESG disclosure quality among Taiwan-listed companies**, enabling cross-industry comparison and identifying patterns in disclosure practices across Environmental, Social, and Governance dimensions.

### C. Practical Contribution
Offers a **scalable and auditable assessment tool** for regulators, investors, and firms to benchmark ESG disclosure quality, improve transparency, and support more reliable sustainability reporting in Taiwan’s capital market.

---

## 4) Three Limitations in Existing Literature / Research Gaps

### 1. Overreliance on Rule-Based Disclosure Metrics
Much of the ESG disclosure literature relies on **keyword counts, checklist scoring, or proprietary ESG ratings**, which often fail to capture context, nuance, and substantive quality of disclosure.

### 2. Limited Application to Non-English and Taiwan Contexts
There is a **lack of research applying advanced NLP or LLM methods to Taiwanese listed firms**, especially for disclosures written in Chinese or mixed-language reporting formats, where translation and contextual interpretation matter.

### 3. Weak Validation Against Human Judgment
Existing studies rarely validate automated ESG disclosure measures against **expert-coded benchmarks**, leaving uncertainty about whether the scores reflect actual disclosure quality rather than surface-level textual features.
