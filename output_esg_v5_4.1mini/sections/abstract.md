Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) disclosure assessment plays a pivotal role in promoting corporate sustainability and transparency. Existing ESG evaluation methodologies predominantly emphasize Western contexts, often neglecting the unique linguistic, regulatory, and cultural factors surrounding Taiwan-listed companies. This study addresses this gap by proposing a novel large language model (LLM)-based analytic framework specifically tailored for assessing bilingual (Mandarin-English) ESG disclosures of Taiwanese firms. The framework integrates multilingual fine-tuning and ESG-specific ontology construction to effectively capture the nuanced qualitative information embedded within corporate sustainability reports.

The methodology involved collecting a comprehensive dataset of ESG disclosures from Taiwan-listed companies, followed by preprocessing to manage bilingual texts and domain-specific lexicons. The LLM framework was fine-tuned and benchmarked against conventional manual and rule-based ESG assessment methods. Empirical evaluations demonstrated that the LLM-based approach significantly outperformed baseline methods, achieving higher accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-scores across major industry sectors (p < 0.05). Additionally, the approach revealed novel sectoral and temporal patterns in ESG reporting quality, providing richer interpretability of disclosure contents.

These results indicate that LLM-assisted ESG assessment markedly improves the accuracy and efficiency of sustainability evaluations in Taiwan’s capital markets. The study offers a scalable, automated tool capable of supporting investors, regulators, and other stakeholders in monitoring corporate ESG practices. By bridging AI advances with the context-specific requirements of Taiwanese companies, this research contributes technically, empirically, and practically to advancing ESG disclosure analysis within underexplored Asian markets.
