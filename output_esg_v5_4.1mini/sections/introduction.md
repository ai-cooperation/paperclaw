The widespread recognition of Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) factors as critical drivers of sustainable business performance and responsible investment has profoundly influenced corporate disclosure practices worldwide. ESG disclosures serve as pivotal communication channels through which firms provide stakeholders—such as investors, regulators, and the public—with insights into their sustainability strategies, risk management, and ethical conduct. The escalating demand for transparent and comprehensive ESG information has led to substantial academic and practical efforts aimed at developing robust methodologies to assess disclosure quality and performance (DueasOcampo2023; Wang2022). Nevertheless, while such studies have been predominantly concentrated on Western capital markets and English-language reports, comparatively less attention has been devoted to Asian contexts, especially Taiwan, where a unique blend of linguistic, regulatory, and cultural features shapes ESG reporting dynamics.

Taiwan’s capital markets are characterized by bilingual disclosures encompassing Mandarin and English, compliance with distinct regulatory frameworks, and cultural factors influencing corporate communication styles (Lai2019; Wang2020). These complexities render ESG disclosure assessment within the Taiwanese environment a nontrivial task. Conventional ESG evaluation approaches, typically predicated on manual review or rule-based systems, are often inefficient, lack consistency, and fail to capture the nuanced qualitative information embedded in textual disclosures (GarcaDaz2020; AllalChrif2021). Furthermore, manual methods do not scale well with the increasing volume and complexity of ESG reports and are susceptible to subjective biases. In particular, existing automated or semi-automated ESG assessment tools inadequately accommodate bilingualism and contextual subtleties prevalent in Taiwanese corporate reports, thereby undermining the accuracy and comprehensiveness of evaluations (Bender2021; DueasOcampo2023).

In recent years, advances in Natural Language Processing (NLP), and notably Large Language Models (LLMs), have revolutionized the capacity to analyze vast amounts of unstructured textual data with high fidelity. LLMs, pre-trained on massive corpora, demonstrate powerful capabilities in capturing contextual semantics, managing multilingual inputs, and generating domain-relevant outputs (Bender2021; GarcaDaz2020). Such features render them promising candidates for enhancing ESG disclosure assessments, which inherently require sophisticated text understanding across regulatory and linguistic boundaries. Despite this potential, the application of LLMs to ESG disclosures remains in an emergent stage, with extant research focusing primarily on English-language financial documents and Western regulatory contexts (AllalChrif2021; Bender2021). Taiwan’s bilingual and culturally specific disclosure environment presents additional challenges that necessitate customized LLM adaptation, including fine-tuning on relevant datasets, incorporation of ESG-specific lexicons, and treatment of mixed-language texts.

Addressing these challenges is critical for several reasons. First, improved ESG assessment accuracy will support more reliable evaluations of Taiwan-listed companies’ sustainability performance, facilitating better-informed investment decisions and regulatory oversight. Second, automated handling of qualitative ESG disclosures can enhance scalability and timeliness, enabling stakeholders to monitor evolving ESG practices effectively. Third, generating empirical insights from Taiwan’s market contributes to diversifying the geographic and linguistic scope of ESG disclosure research, which is essential for global sustainability initiatives (DueasOcampo2023; Belkahla2019). This study, therefore, fills notable gaps in current literature and practice by leveraging the recent advances in LLM technology tailored to the Taiwanese ESG disclosure context.

The contributions of this study are threefold. First, a technical contribution is made through the development of an LLM-based analytic framework specifically designed for bilingual Mandarin-English ESG disclosures of Taiwan-listed companies. This framework integrates multilingual fine-tuning strategies and domain-specific ESG ontology construction to effectively capture the linguistic nuances and regulatory characteristics unique to Taiwan’s corporate ESG ecosystem. Second, the study provides an empirical contribution by applying the LLM framework to a comprehensive dataset of ESG disclosures from Taiwan-listed firms, enabling the extraction of novel insights into sectoral disclosure patterns, temporal trends, and qualitative ESG content. Third, a practical contribution results from delivering a scalable, automated ESG assessment tool prototype that stakeholders—including investors and regulators—can employ to efficiently and consistently evaluate ESG practices within Taiwan’s capital markets, thus promoting enhanced sustainability transparency and decision-making support.

The remainder of this paper is structured as follows. Section 2 reviews pertinent literature on ESG disclosure assessment methodologies, challenges specific to Asian and Taiwanese contexts, and the current state of AI and LLM applications in financial and ESG domains. Section 3 details the methodology, including data collection, preprocessing, the LLM framework design, and validation procedures. Section 4 presents the experimental results, comparing the LLM-based assessment with manual and rule-based baselines, along with sectoral and temporal analyses. Section 5 discusses the implications of findings, limitations of the approach, and avenues for future research. Finally, Section 6 concludes by summarizing the key contributions and practical significance of the study for advancing ESG disclosure assessment in Taiwan and similar contexts.

In summary, this study addresses the pressing need for advanced, context-aware ESG assessment frameworks in Taiwan’s bilingual and regulatory environment by employing state-of-the-art LLM technology. By overcoming the limitations of existing manual and rule-based methods, the research advances both theoretical understanding and practical capabilities in ESG disclosure evaluation, ultimately contributing to more sustainable business strategies and enhanced corporate transparency within Taiwan’s capital markets.
