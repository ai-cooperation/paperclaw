The findings demonstrate that the proposed LLM-based analytic framework markedly enhances ESG disclosure assessment for Taiwan-listed companies, outperforming manual and rule-based methods in accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score across multiple industry sectors. By integrating multilingual fine-tuning and ESG-specific ontologies, the model effectively captures the nuanced bilingual (Mandarin-English) expressions and regulatory context unique to the Taiwanese market, addressing a critical gap in existing ESG evaluation research [@Bender2021; @GarcaDaz2020; @DueasOcampo2023]. The empirical analyses reveal meaningful sectoral and temporal trends in ESG reporting quality, underscoring the framework’s capability to extract rich qualitative insights from unstructured corporate disclosures. This study’s automated approach significantly reduces processing time while maintaining interpretability, thereby providing a scalable tool to support investors, regulators, and other stakeholders in making more informed, timely decisions [@AllalChrif2021].

Future research may focus on extending the multilingual framework to additional Asian markets with complex linguistic landscapes, enabling cross-market ESG comparisons. Moreover, integrating the LLM-assisted assessment with real-time regulatory reporting systems could enhance continuous monitoring and compliance verification. Finally, investigating methods to mitigate potential biases inherent in training data and exploring more energy-efficient model architectures would advance the responsible deployment of AI in ESG evaluation [@Bender2021; @Kovai2020]. Continued refinement of LLM applications promises to deepen sustainable business strategy insights and accelerate transparency in emerging financial environments.
