# Research Concept Document: LLM-Assisted ESG Disclosure Assessment for Taiwan Listed Companies

## 1. Research Question  
How can large language models (LLMs) be utilized to improve the accuracy, efficiency, and comprehensiveness of ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) disclosure assessments for Taiwan-listed companies?

## 2. Hypothesis  
Integrating LLM technology into ESG disclosure assessment frameworks will significantly enhance the accuracy and depth of ESG evaluations of Taiwan-listed companies compared to traditional manual and rule-based assessment methods.

## 3. Contributions  

### Technical Contribution  
- Development of an LLM-based analytic framework tailored to interpret and assess ESG disclosures in multiple languages (Mandarin and English), addressing the linguistic nuances specific to Taiwan-listed companies.

### Empirical Contribution  
- Comprehensive evaluation of ESG disclosures from Taiwan-listed companies utilizing the LLM framework, providing novel insights into disclosure patterns, sectoral strengths and weaknesses, and temporal changes in ESG reporting quality.

### Practical Contribution  
- Offering a scalable, automated tool for investors, regulators, and other stakeholders to efficiently monitor and assess ESG practices, supporting more informed decision-making and fostering greater corporate transparency in Taiwan’s capital markets.

## 4. Limitations in Existing Literature (Research Gaps)  
- **Lack of Focus on Taiwan Context:** Existing ESG assessment studies predominantly focus on Western markets, with limited attention to the unique regulatory, linguistic, and cultural context of Taiwan-listed companies.  
- **Underexplored Use of LLMs in ESG Disclosure Analysis:** While LLMs have been applied in various financial document analyses, their application for ESG disclosure evaluation remains underdeveloped, especially in non-English contexts.  
- **Insufficient Automation of Qualitative ESG Data Processing:** Traditional ESG assessments rely heavily on manual review or structured quantitative data, leading to inefficiencies and potential biases when handling the unstructured qualitative ESG disclosures prevalent in corporate reports.

## 5. Suggested Target Journal and Rationale  

**Journal:** *Business Strategy and the Environment*  
**Rationale:** This journal emphasizes innovative research at the intersection of environmental and social sustainability with business strategy. The study’s focus on leveraging advanced AI technologies (LLMs) for ESG disclosure assessment aligns well with the journal’s mission to explore and promote cutting-edge practices that enhance corporate sustainability performance and transparency. Furthermore, the journal’s international readership, including academics, practitioners, and policymakers, represents an ideal audience for disseminating findings relevant to the Taiwanese market and beyond.
