## 1. Section Outline

- **Key Points:**

  - Brief description of the developed LLM-based ESG assessment framework  
  - Summary of empirical evaluation results comparing LLM and human analyst assessments  
  - Practical implications and potential impact on stakeholders

- **References to Cite:** Cheng2020 (for ESG importance/context), Permatasari2021 (AI in ESG), Jeyaraj2022 (AI adoption challenges)

## 4. Research Contract

| Element       | Details                                                       |
|---------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Research Questions** | 1. How effectively can LLMs assist in assessing ESG disclosures of Taiwan-listed companies? <br> 2. What is the impact of LLM use on accuracy, consistency, and timeliness of ESG reporting evaluation compared to manual methods? <br> 3. What practical challenges exist for adoption of LLM tools in ESG assessment workflows? |
| **Hypotheses** | LLM-assisted ESG disclosure assessments significantly improve accuracy, consistency, and timeliness versus traditional manual evaluations in Taiwan’s ESG context. |
| **Methods**    | - Development of a customized LLM-based ESG assessment framework fine-tuned for Taiwan-specific language and sector terms <br> - Empirical evaluation comparing LLM outputs to human analyst assessments on a curated dataset of Taiwan-listed companies’ ESG reports <br> - Quantitative benchmarking using accuracy, consistency, and timeliness metrics <br> - Optional stakeholder survey or interviews to explore practical adoption barriers and acceptance |
| **Expected Outcomes** | - Validation that LLM assistance improves ESG assessment quality and speed <br> - Identification of ESG disclosure elements best captured by LLMs and areas of limitations <br> - Actionable guidelines and recommendations for corporate sustainability officers, investors, and regulators on integrating AI tools effectively for ESG evaluation |
| **Limitations** | - Dataset limited to Taiwan-listed companies; generalizability to other regions or broader Asian markets may require further research <br> - Potential language and domain adaptation challenges with specific ESG terminologies <br> - User acceptance insights may be preliminary without large-scale organizational implementation data |

This detailed structural plan ensures coherent development of a high-quality empirical paper addressing the novel intersection of LLMs and ESG assessment in an emerging Asian market context for the targeted journal *Business Strategy and the Environment*.
